excerpt: "High-fidelity simulations for high-velocity impact rely on material models and parameters that are often calibrated through labor-intensive manual fitting. This work develops an ensemble-based data assimilation framework that integrates Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics, the ensemble Kalman filter, and adaptive covariance inflation to automatically identify representative plasticity, fracture, and equation-of-state parameters from a single high-velocity impact test. Using synthetic back-face deflection data of an AZ31B magnesium plate, the study shows that the proposed approach can efficiently recover sensitive parameters, diagnose identifiability through ensemble statistics, and provide a robust alternative to traditional calibration workflows."

excerpt: "Rigorous safety certification under partial uncertainty requires bounding failure probabilities using limited statistical information. This work develops an optimal uncertainty quantification framework for statistically independent inputs constrained by general truncated moments over subdomains. The proposed formulation converts the original infinite-dimensional optimization over probability measures into finite-dimensional unconstrained problems parameterized by free canonical moments, and incorporates inverse transform sampling to reduce the cost of probability-of-failure evaluation in high-dimensional settings. Numerical examples, including a ten-dimensional ballistic impact problem, show that additional subdomain and moment information systematically tightens the optimal bounds, while the sampling strategy reduces computational cost by up to two orders of magnitude with less than 1% relative error. The results also identify when subdomain partitioning or higher-order moments most effectively improve conservative safety certification."
